## Phase Goal

**As a** pilot, **I want to** fly my ship through a star system and approach dithered planets and a star while reading my speed and surroundings on a retro HUD, **so that** I can navigate a true 1:1 cosmos that stays correct and feels good at every scale.

## MVP Slice — Flight Feel

This slice replaces the skeleton's single placeholder thrust key with the real arcade flight model: a visible virtual-joystick steering reticle, persistent throttle, roll, hold-attitude stabilization, and distance-scaled speed that makes the 1:1 cosmos traversable (crawl near bodies, FTL-fast in open space). After this plan, a real user can actually *fly* — pitch/yaw toward where they want to go, hold a throttle, and feel the ship slow near a planet and accelerate away — none of which the skeleton's straight-line thrust could do. The fixed crosshair + moving reticle (HUD-03/D-05) make steering legible.

<objective>
Create a `FlightController` node implementing the locked arcade model: a visible accumulated-cursor virtual joystick (D-01/D-05) that rotates the ship toward the reticle with drift-free `Basis` composition and clean hold-attitude (D-02), roll on Q/E (D-04), persistent throttle with a full-stop key (D-03), and a distance-to-nearest-surface speed envelope eased frame-to-frame so there is no SOI-boundary snap (D-06/D-07/D-08). It computes a forward `Double3` delta and drives `GameWorld.TranslatePos`. Add the fixed center crosshair + moving steering reticle (HUD-03).

Purpose: Make the 1:1 star system genuinely flyable with arcade feel — the core verb of the game.

<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

Single-player offline; no network/auth/persistence. The flight controller is the ONLY mutator of simulation state, so its boundary into `TranslatePos` is the one place a bad value could destabilize the sim.

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| mouse/keyboard input → `FlightController` | Raw input deltas accumulate into cursor/attitude/throttle; unbounded accumulation could produce extreme deltas |
| `FlightController` → `GameWorld.TranslatePos` | The computed `Double3` delta is the sole sim mutation; a NaN/huge delta drives multi-SOI transitions |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-03-01 | Denial of Service (local) | unbounded attitude `Basis` drift over many frames | mitigate | `Orthonormalized()` every frame prevents skew accumulation (Task 1) — keeps attitude valid, no uncontrolled spin |
| T-03-02 | Denial of Service (local) | huge/NaN motion delta into `TranslatePos` triggering pathological transition | mitigate | Speed clamped to `[MinSpeed, MaxSpeed]` and eased (Task 2); the STAB-01 `MaxIterations` cap (Plan 01) bounds transition cost even for a large single-frame delta |
| T-03-03 | Denial of Service (local) | cursor accumulation overflow | mitigate | `_cursor.LimitLength(MaxCursorRadius)` clamps the software cursor every input event (Task 1) |
| T-03-04 | Tampering | throttle out of [0,1] range | mitigate | Throttle clamped to [0,1]; `Mathf.Clamp` guard on the `[Export]` step (Task 1) |

No network, auth, session, secrets, or cryptography surface in this phase.
</threat_model>
