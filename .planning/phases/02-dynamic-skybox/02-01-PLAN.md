<objective>
Deliver the first end-to-end vertical slice of the dynamic skybox: real authored sibling star systems become world-fixed, dithered light points on the sky while the ship flies in-system. This single slice spans authored data (D-23/D-26), pure re-tier logic (TierClassifier), the per-frame read-only renderer (SkyboxRenderer), the sky shader (skybox.gdshader), and the Main.tscn wiring that activates the Sky background.

Purpose: Prove the core RND-05 mechanism — world-fixed direction projection via EYEDIR, classification of next-tier-out bodies, and automatic dither pass-through — against the real engine before layering magnitude ranking (Plan 02) and unit tests (Plan 03).

Output: After this plan, launching the scene and flying to a space whose next tier out holds the sibling stars shows them as discrete points that stay pinned to world space as the ship rotates and are quantized by the existing dithering shader.
</objective>

## Phase Goal

**As a** pilot flying in-system, **I want to** see the real stars of neighbouring star systems as distant fixed light points on the sky, **so that** the cosmos around me feels vast and the next places I can travel to are visibly out there.
