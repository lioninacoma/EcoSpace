<objective>
Close the two BLOCKER gaps found in Phase 8 verification (08-VERIFICATION.md) without
touching already-verified behavior from 08-01 / 08-02.

BLOCKER 1 (WR-02): `_WarpProcess` applies `CurrentSpeed * delta` displacement with no clamp
relative to remaining distance to the SOI boundary. A single oversized frame (hitch / alt-tab /
breakpoint) at cruise speed (~3e20 m/s) displaces the ship ~1.5e19 m past PlanetSOI=1e9 m in one
step; warp then never re-arrives and the ship drifts away forever. ROADMAP WR-02 prescribed
"Clamp the per-frame step to the SOI arrival point."

BLOCKER 2 (P3-TIMING): `WarpMotionProfile.Solve()` clamps `vCruise` to 0 when D < rampContribution
(target just outside its SOI) but leaves the ramps running at vLaunch=vTerminal=1e6, so the
velocity integral ≈ rampContrib ≫ D (a ~40,000× overshoot). The area=D / exact-arrival-time
invariant breaks in the small-D regime, and the only small-D test asserts finiteness, so the
regression is invisible.

Purpose: Restore the two headline guarantees — exact T_sel arrival across ALL scales, and
no SOI tunneling on a frame boundary.
Output: A failing-then-passing small-D regression test, an infeasible-case fix in Solve,
and a per-frame displacement clamp + timeout backstop in _WarpProcess.
</objective>
