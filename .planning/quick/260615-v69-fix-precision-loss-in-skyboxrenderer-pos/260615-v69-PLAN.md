<objective>
Replace SkyboxRenderer's absolute-from-root position accumulation with a lowest-common-ancestor
(LCA) relative walk so the ship→body vector is formed without ever constructing an
absolute-from-root coordinate. Both the ship and the body are walked up to their LCA and expressed
in the LCA's frame; the subtraction happens there, so the LCA's (potentially huge) absolute
position never enters the arithmetic and catastrophic cancellation of two large nearly-equal
vectors is eliminated.

This is precision-hardening, NOT a behavior change. In the MVP scene the LCA of the ship and any
sibling star is the Galaxy (index 1), and Galaxy + Root both sit at the origin, so every output
(dir3, _skyDirs, len) must remain numerically identical within float tolerance to today's results.

Purpose: harden the file that builds the RND-07/D-21 tier-handoff baseline. Precision is weakest
exactly at the tier boundary and degrades at the 1:1 Universe scale (1e16 m/unit) the project
targets. Subtracting near the LCA keeps the operands small at every scale.

Output: a revised Scripts/Render/SkyboxRenderer.cs — single file, read-only, same public API.
</objective>

<design_notes>
Hierarchy (from TestSetup): Root(0) → Galaxy(1, Universe space) → Star(2, Galaxy space) →
PlanetA(3)/PlanetB(4) (Star space) → Ship(5, Planet space). Sibling stars (6,7,8) are children of
Galaxy, in Galaxy space. So LCA(ship, sibling) = Galaxy. Galaxy.LocalPos = (0,0,0) at Universe
scale and Root sits at origin → today's absolute-from-root math already yields the correct relative
vector, which is why outputs must match exactly.

CRITICAL precision caveat the implementation MUST honor (from UniVec3):
- UniVec3.Convert(), operator-, operator+ all route through EnsureSameScale → Convert → ToDouble3(),
  which ZEROES the Long3 Units (folds everything into a single double). So a naive
  `bodyUni - shipUni` across spaces collapses to double and throws away the precision split.
- The precision win therefore comes ONLY from anchoring the walk at the LCA: each side accumulates
  its offset DOWN-from / relative-to the LCA in a single consistent basis whose magnitude is bounded
  by the inter-body separation, not by the absolute-from-root distance. The large common offset is
  never formed because the walk stops at the LCA on both sides.

Chosen basis: meters, with the LCA as the local origin. Accumulate each side's position relative to
the LCA by walking child→parent and converting child-frame meters into the parent frame
(childMeters expressed in parent space = childPos.ToDouble3()-in-child-frame, then the parent's own
offset added at each step — exactly the per-step pattern WorldRenderer.ComputeStarRenderPosFromHierarchy
uses, generalized to walk to an arbitrary ancestor instead of one fixed level). Stop each walk when
the next parent index equals the LCA index. Subtract the two LCA-frame meter vectors (small: bounded
by body separation) and only THEN form dir3 / len. The subtraction is a Double3 subtraction of two
small vectors, so no cancellation of huge operands.

Why meters (not UniVec3 Units arithmetic for the subtract): the two sides may originate in different
spaces/scales (ship in Planet space scale=1e-4; sibling in Galaxy space scale=1e4). Expressing both
relative to the SAME LCA frame in meters gives one consistent basis. The values stay small because
they are measured from the LCA, not root — that is the precision guarantee, independent of which
scalar basis is used for the small final delta. (Per-step accumulation uses
Units*Scale+Offset in the step's own frame, where each step's value is in-range for double.)

LCA discovery: build the ancestor chain of one node (e.g. ship) via ParentIndex up to root, recording
each index; then walk the other node (body) up via ParentIndex until the first index that appears in
the ship's chain — that is the LCA. Bound every loop by (uint)idx < (uint)objs.Count and null guards
(existing idiom). Hierarchy depth is tiny (≤5), so a small stack/HashSet or even two passes is fine.

Edge cases to preserve / handle:
- LCA == body or LCA == ship (one is an ancestor of the other): the ancestor side contributes zero
  offset from the LCA frame (it IS the LCA), the descendant side contributes its walk down. The
  subtraction still works. TierClassifier guarantees body is in an ANCESTOR space of the ship for
  NextTierSkybox, but do not rely on that for safety — handle the general LCA result.
- No common ancestor found (should be impossible — all share Root): fall back to Vector3.Up so the
  method never throws and never produces NaN (mirror the existing zero-length guard spirit).
- Root has Scale = -1; the existing AbsolutePositionInRoot stops when pScale <= 0. The LCA walk
  stops at the LCA (which is never Root for sky bodies), so the Scale<=0 guard is naturally avoided,
  but keep a defensive check so a Root-scaled step is never multiplied in.
</design_notes>
